# LRPM (Loaded Revenue per Mile) — Week Ending 2026-02-15

**Period**: 2025-08-02 to 2026-02-15 (26 weeks)

## Executive Summary

Loaded Revenue per Mile (LRPM) across the Central region has declined 4.8% over 26 weeks, with the most acute drops concentrated in Amarillo (-11.2%) and El Paso (-8.7%). The network decline reflects both rate compression in the spot market and a shift toward shorter-haul loads. Albuquerque is the only terminal showing LRPM improvement (+3.1%), attributed to higher-value load mix.

## Top Findings

1. **Amarillo — LRPM Deterioration Accelerating** (HIGH): LRPM fell from $2.41 to $2.14 over 26 weeks (-11.2%). The decline accelerated in the last 6 weeks (avg weekly decline: -0.012 vs -0.004 in prior 20 weeks). 42 trucks at risk.

2. **El Paso — Structural LRPM Weakness** (HIGH): LRPM at $1.98, lowest in Central region. YoY decline of -8.7%. Change point detected in Oct 2025 coincides with a reported routing change.

3. **Albuquerque — Lone Bright Spot** (MEDIUM): Despite volume decline, remaining Albuquerque trucks achieve $2.68 LRPM, up from $2.60 baseline. Suggests selective load acceptance as fleet shrinks.
